 Study tool recommendation - from Ardak:
“Andrey K” - Youtube Channel

 Let’s be honest: attending lectures, taking notes, and listening carefully to professors are crucial, but for biotechnology and genomics students, they’re rarely enough. To truly grasp and retain complex material, self-study plays a huge role.
 However, reading textbooks or going through long presentations can be time-consuming, especially during exam season.

 That’s why I’d like to recommend the “Andrey K” YouTube channel. Even though it’s no longer actively updated, the existing videos remain incredibly useful.
 They offer quick yet detailed explanations of many biological and chemical processes, all supported by helpful visuals and diagrams. The way topics are broken down makes them much easier to understand and remember.

 Personally, I often turn to this channel before exams for a quick refresher. As a Genomics student, my studies combine biology, chemistry, statistics, programming, and more. It’s a lot to keep in your head over multiple semesters, especially as new topics start to build on past ones.
 That’s where this channel helps: it brings older content back into focus in a simple and engaging way.

 For example, while preparing for my metabolomics exam last semester, I used one of the videos to review the citric acid cycle -a topic full of enzymes, intermediate compounds, and energy transfers that can be hard to recall in detail.
 The clear structure and visuals made the process much easier to understand and remember.

 If you're looking for a fast, visual, and reliable way to reinforce your understanding, I highly recommend checking out “Andrey K” on YouTube. It’s a great tool for refreshing your memory and boosting your confidence before exams.

 Each month, we will highlight one of the research areas our department is involved in, aiming to encourage you to read, explore and discover your own interests.

 This month, we focus on genomics —a relatively recent but rapidly growing research field.
 One notable piece of recent news in this domain involves the company 23andMe, which filed for bankruptcy this month.
 For those unfamiliar with the company, 23andMe is best known for its direct-to-consumer genetic testing service. Customers provide a saliva sample that is analyzed in a laboratory using single nucleotide polymorphism (SNP) genotyping to generate reports on ancestry and genetic predispositions to various health-related conditions.

 The primary controversy surrounding 23andMe has been related to privacy concerns. Genetic information is highly sensitive, revealing not only personal details but also information about one's family. Such data should only be shared with trustworthy entities.
 Unfortunately, 23andMe failed to be a such entity, making a profit from the private information of its many customers, without even giving them much useful results. It’s also worth mentioning the data breach from 2023 when they were hacked, and the information of 7 million of their customers got leaked.

 To learn more about this important story, we highly recommend you to check out this insightful Guardian article.
